Master Mobile UX Sitepoint Course

Doing large frst rather than mobile first is like trying to put a marshmallow into a piggy bank.

The number of devices as well as turnover devices have changed drastically with mobile devices. Mobile traffic has increased greatly, hence MOBILE FIRST importance.

Responsive doesn't always mean mobile first. Retrofitting is making your site work with mobile. It may be a good temporary alternative to a rebuild if necessary.

    Things to Remember:
  • Desktop computer sales have been declining since 2011
  • You don't see people with their heads in laptops
  • Start small and scale up
  • Mobile is not a fad

People don't know what they want.

Be There, Be Useful, Be Quick

Reachability matters - with Mobile first. Think of one handed and two handed grip. What is hard to get to?

Speed Matters - slow and more clicks is a drop in traffic, revenue and overal useage.

Reduce images, use SVG/Webfonts, optimize and minify css and js files, use gzip files to eliminate unecessary data.

Be empathetic to users and consider primary controls of mobile devices. Good enough isn't good enough! This includes reachability, placement, and consideration of universal understanding.

For forms simplicity and required information is important to consider. "Reduce cognitive load".

Difficulty in forms often leads to complete abandonment of the task.

    Useful tips:
  • Auto-complete
  • Using intutive design
  • like auto progress credit card information like in the example
  • Auto progress through form
  • Password difficulty consideration
  • Number only input consideration (switch to number only keyboard)
  • Phone numbers with a link (rather than causing them to copy/paste)

Advertising can be evolved with technology.

Pop ups should be avoided.

If you place ads in content, make them small! You can make them small at first and grow once clicked.

Be creative with ads like using a background window that shows when scrolling.

Users don't visit your website to look at ads.

Content should come first.
